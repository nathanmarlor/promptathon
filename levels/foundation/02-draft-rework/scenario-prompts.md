# Drafting Scenario Prompts

Use these scenarios to practise drafting communications from scratch.

---

## Scenario 1: Project Delay Announcement

**Situation:** You need to inform stakeholders that a key project will be delayed by two weeks due to a dependency on an external vendor.

**Key facts:**
- Original deadline: February 15
- New deadline: March 1
- Cause: Vendor API delivery was late
- Impact: No budget impact, but delays downstream testing
- Mitigation: Team is running parallel workstreams where possible

**Audience:** Project steering committee (senior stakeholders)

**Goal:** Inform them of the delay while maintaining confidence in the team

---

## Scenario 2: New Policy Implementation

**Situation:** You need to announce a new expense approval policy that requires pre-approval for any expense over £500.

**Key facts:**
- Effective date: First of next month
- Previous threshold: £1,000
- Reason: Audit findings recommended tighter controls
- Process: Submit via expense system, 48-hour turnaround for approvals
- Exception: Pre-approved recurring expenses (software subscriptions, etc.)

**Audience:** All people managers in your division

**Goal:** Ensure compliance while minimising frustration

---

## Scenario 3: Team Restructure

**Situation:** You need to communicate that your team is being reorganised. No one is losing their job, but reporting lines are changing.

**Key facts:**
- Three teams becoming two teams
- All roles preserved, some people getting new managers
- Effective in two weeks
- New structure aligns team to customer segments rather than product lines
- Individual conversations happening this week

**Audience:** Your entire department (50 people)

**Goal:** Explain the change clearly, reduce anxiety, signal that details will follow

---

## Scenario 4: Declining a Request

**Situation:** A colleague has asked you to support their proposal at an upcoming leadership meeting. You've reviewed it and don't think it's ready—the business case needs more work.

**Key facts:**
- You respect this colleague and want to maintain the relationship
- The proposal has merit but the financials are weak
- You'd be willing to help them strengthen it
- The leadership meeting is in two weeks

**Audience:** A peer you work with regularly

**Goal:** Decline supportively while offering constructive help

---

## Scenario 5: Congratulating Achievement

**Situation:** One of your team members just closed the largest deal in the company's history. You want to recognise this publicly.

**Key facts:**
- Deal value: £2.4M annual contract
- Sales cycle: 14 months
- The person involved: Jamie (has been with company 3 years)
- This surpasses the previous record by 40%
- Several team members supported the pursuit

**Audience:** Company-wide announcement

**Goal:** Celebrate the win, recognise the individual and supporting team, reinforce company values

---

## How to Use These Scenarios

1. Pick a scenario relevant to your experience
2. Draft a prompt using the CLEAR framework
3. Generate initial content with Copilot
4. Iterate to improve the output
5. Reflect on what worked and what needed adjustment
