# Presentation Briefs

Use these scenarios to practise creating presentations with Copilot.

---

## Brief 1: Strategy Update

**Presentation Type:** Quarterly strategy update  
**Audience:** All employees (150 people)  
**Duration:** 20 minutes  
**Purpose:** Inform and align

**Key Messages:**
1. Q3 performance exceeded targets (+12% revenue)
2. We're adjusting Q4 priorities based on market changes
3. Three initiatives launching next quarter
4. Everyone has a role in the next phase

**Content to Include:**
- Performance highlights (3-4 metrics)
- Market context (2-3 trends affecting us)
- Strategic priorities (3 items)
- What this means for different teams
- Timeline for next quarter
- Q&A

**Tone:** Confident, forward-looking, inclusive

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Create a 12-slide strategy update presentation for all employees.

Key points:
- Q3 exceeded targets by 12%
- Market conditions changing (include 2-3 trends)
- Three new initiatives for Q4: [list them]
- How this affects different teams

Structure: Performance → Context → Strategy → Your Role → Timeline → Q&A

Tone: Confident and inclusive. Celebrate wins but focus on the future.
Each slide: one headline message, 3 bullets max.
Include speaker notes.
```

## Brief 2: Project Proposal

**Presentation Type:** Investment approval request  
**Audience:** Executive committee (6 people)  
**Duration:** 15 minutes + 15 minutes Q&A  
**Purpose:** Get approval for £500K investment

**Key Messages:**
1. Problem/opportunity is significant (quantify)
2. Our solution is the best approach
3. Investment is justified (ROI case)
4. Risks are manageable
5. We have a credible plan

**Content to Include:**
- Problem statement with business impact
- Solution overview
- Options considered (why this one?)
- Financial case (investment, returns, payback)
- Implementation plan and timeline
- Risk assessment
- Ask and approval needed

**Tone:** Business-focused, confident, evidence-based

**Sample Prompt:**
```
Create a 10-slide investment proposal for executive approval.

Structure:
1. Title
2. The problem and business impact (quantified)
3. Our proposed solution
4. Why this approach vs alternatives
5-6. Financial case (investment, ROI, payback)
7. Implementation timeline
8. Risks and mitigations
9. The ask (what approval is needed)
10. Q&A

Audience: Executives who are supportive but need evidence.
Focus on business outcomes, not technical details.
Include speaker notes with likely questions.
```

## Brief 3: Team Onboarding

**Presentation Type:** New team member orientation  
**Audience:** New hires (3-5 people)  
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Purpose:** Inform and engage

**Key Messages:**
1. Welcome—you made a great choice
2. Here's what we do and why it matters
3. Here's how we work
4. Here's how to succeed
5. Here are your resources

**Content to Include:**
- Team mission and purpose
- How we fit in the organisation
- Team structure and roles
- How we work (processes, tools, rhythms)
- Key stakeholders
- 30/60/90 day expectations
- Resources and support
- FAQ

**Tone:** Welcoming, clear, practical

## Brief 4: Customer Business Review

**Presentation Type:** Quarterly Business Review (QBR)  
**Audience:** Customer stakeholders (4-6 people)  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Purpose:** Demonstrate value, deepen relationship

**Key Messages:**
1. You're getting strong value from the partnership
2. Here's what we've achieved together
3. Here's what's coming that benefits you
4. We're invested in your success

**Content to Include:**
- Executive summary
- Usage and adoption metrics
- Business outcomes achieved
- Support and service review
- Roadmap items relevant to them
- Recommendations for maximising value
- Next steps

**Tone:** Partnership-focused, outcome-oriented, forward-looking
